Nut and seed loaf
This is a good way to use up assorted nuts and seeds. It's like a large sliceable granola bar: soft, chewy, hearty, and not too sweet.

Ingredients
    Dry
  • 140 g nuts/seeds (1)
  • 100 g raisins (2)
  • 1/2 Tbsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 180 g rolled oats
  • 20 g chia seeds
  • 30 g ground flax seed
  • 20 g psyllium husks

    • Wet
  • 20 g brown sugar
  • 30 g canola oil
  • 360 mL water
Instructions
  1. In a large bowl, stir together all of the dry ingredients.
  2. Add all of the wet ingredients. Stir to combine.
  3. Line a loaf pan with parchment paper. This makes it easier to remove the loaf and clean the pan.
  4. Add the mixture to the pan. Smooth the top with the back of a spoon.
  5. Cover the loaf pan, and let it sit 6 to 24 hours. This helps the loaf absorb liquid and keep its shape. I leave it overnight.
  6. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. Bake for 50 minutes. Let cool completely (~1 hour) before slicing. I cut it into ~12 slices and then cut the slices in half.
  7. Store leftovers in the fridge for up to a week. It also freezes well.
(1) I use 70 g sliced almonds, 70 g hemp hearts. You can also use sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, walnuts, pecans, hazelnuts, etc.

(2) Other dried fruit i.e. dates, figs, apples, blueberries also work. If you have big pieces of nuts or fruit (i.e. a whole walnut or an apple ring), I'd suggest chopping them into raisin-size pieces. This will help the loaf stay together.
